Other beautiful places

As more commissions come my way, I see beauty through other people’s eyes. I have painted scenes all around Ireland – Dublin, Wicklow, Waterford, Limerick, Clare, Donegal, Antrim, along with scenes in Portugal, Australia, Cornwall, and Brighton. Through my painting I hope to expand my horizons and explore many more beautiful places.

Making Memories, Godrevy, Cornwall, England

I painted this for a couple who had holidayed with family at the end of the first lockdown. Having been away from the sea for six months, they asked me to capture all that they love - sea, surf, and sand. This painting hangs in Bristol.

Brighton Pier, England

I painted this commission for someone living in London with special memories of time spent at Brighton. I used pointillism (small dots) for the sand and copper foil and paint for the rusted pillars.

Castle ruins, Newcastle, Co. Wicklow

I was commissioned to paint the Castle ruins at Newcastle by someone who grew up in the town but has emigrated. As it was built in the 12th century and is still standing proud, I felt I had use strong colours and brushstrokes to demonstrate its resilience and history, letting the village, church and Wicklow mountains melt into the background.

The Drummin Trees from Bellevue Hill, Delgany, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

Everyone from Greystones, Delgany and most of Co. Wicklow recognise these iconic trees letting you know you are nearing home. In fact, during the First World War when trees were being cut down for fuel, these trees escaped such fate as they were used as a navigation aid for pilots. I was commissioned to paint this stunning scene from Bellevue Hill by someone who lives in Delgany and wanted the view captured inside as well as when out walking.

Ardmore Co. Waterford - Childhood Memories - Ardmore beach, Co. Waterford, Ireland

A friend commissioned me to paint this scene from a photo she sent me to celebrate her cousin's big birthday. She explained that Ardmore was her cousin's 'Greystones' - full of special moments and memories. This was my first painting of a seascape other than Greystones. It was very well received and gave me the confidence to know that once I listen to what the client wants I don't have to be there to be able to deliver.

Time to Breath - Ards, Creeslough Co. Donegal, Ireland.

This painting was for a client who wanted me to capture the essence of peace this view gives her when walking the stretch of land. The views across the peninsula to Donegal in the distance and the sand and sea below makes it a very special place to visit.

Totally Abstract.

When I painted this and posted it on social media (my first truly abstract painting) I was overwhelmed with the response. Everyone sees something different - swans, boats, marshland, Athens, coastline, to name but a few! What do you see?

The Octagon Glen the Downs, Co. Wicklow, Ireland.

Back in the day The Octagon at Glen of the Downs, Co. Wicklow was part of the Bellevue estate, where it was a hunting lodge and tea room for the landed gentry. For youngsters today the ruin and magical trees keep the secrets of teenage love locked away safe and sound! I painted this for a couple who carved their love into the trees more than 40 years ago!

Fairyland at Glen of the Downs, Co, Wicklow, Ireland

There is something so magical about late autumn in the woods. My cousin took a photo of Glen of the Downs, Co. Wicklow in November, and I just had to paint it, capturing the ancient trees losing their leaves and the sunny sky above. The original painting found its home in California.

Great Sugar Loaf, Co Wicklow, Ireland

The iconic conical shape of this glorious mountain tells those flying into Dublin airport that they are nearly home. I so enjoyed painting this scene knowing it will be recognised and loved by returners to Ireland, those driving down the M11, and people walking and cycling in the Wicklow mountains. I painted this to help raise funds for the DWMRT - Dublin, Wicklow Mountain Rescue Team.
