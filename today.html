Greystones Today

Much has changed in Greystones over the years, and I love capturing the new beauty in a more concrete landscape. All we need are grey stones, changing seas, tides, winds (maybe some sun!), with the Sugarloaf mountains, Bray Head and Kindlestown woods forming the protective backdrop to the Greystones community.

Autumn Concerto in Sea - Stormy October at Greystones cove, Co. Wicklow, Ireland.

October brings stormy days and stormy waves. Capturing the sea's force was great fun. This has proved to be a very popular painting request. Originals hang in homes in Greystones, Delgany, Dublin, America, and Germany. I used the pointallism (small dots) technique with gems to bring texture to the pebbly sand, and flicking white paint with a toothbrush makes great spray!

Waves with Attitude, North beach, Greystones, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

When the new marina development started to take shape I realised that this is the view many residents would wake to, with the north beach making its presence felt and Bray Head wrapping protectively around it. I shared my painting with the estate agent selling the homes who bought greeting cards to put in the welcome pack for new home owners. The original painting was bought by a Greystones resident.

Rocky 'Stones, Greystones seafront, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

Greystones, as the name says, is all about the 'Stones. This iconic seafront view was bought for a 70th birthday present. I used the pointillism technique (small dots) to create texture in the rocks and the sand, and the afternoon sun casts shadows on the sand.

Safe and sound, Greystones Harbour, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

Love it or loath it, the new harbour is here to stay, so I wanted to show it off in its best light with the calm waters and blue skies. I used the pointallism technique (small dots) to create texture in the sand, and collage to create the pier. It hangs in a Spendloves coffee and ice cream shop.

The view from my school, Greystones seafront, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

I went to St. David's Junior and secondary school. Sitting in class looking out at this view was a beautiful distraction (and slipping out for a quick lunchtime swim a rite of passage!). I was commissioned to paint this by someone who shared the same memories and still remains my friend.

Wind of Change, Acrylic painting of Greystones South Beach looking towards the Men's, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

As the topography of the coastline changes there are now only a few days each year, when the north-east winds blow, when the Men's of old is revealed in all its glory. I painted this from a photo taken last autumn. I used the pointillism (small dots) technique to bring texture to the stony south beach. The foam reminds me of meringue mixture!

This is us - Greystones Cove, Greystones, Co. Wicklow

I have painted this scene many times with slight variations. This commission was a 50th birthday present from two friends to another, all of whom swim at the cove all year round. Two of the friends cycle to the cove for their swim and they wanted me to capture this in the painting.

Watching Over, Greystones Cove, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

This is a special view that evokes a range of emotions for many people. I was first commissioned to paint it for a someone who had very happy memories of swimming with all her Greystones friends as a child and teenager. Sadly, one friend had passed away, but she wanted me to capture her presence in the painting. I subsequently had similar requests but with different swimmers.

Perfectly Greystones, the Cove and the Club, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

This was my very first painting, done in December 2018, so has a special place in my heart. When I come home to Greystones this is the first place I go, to look to Bray Head to the north, and the Men's and south beach to the south. What more could I want?

Searching and Soaring, Greystones North Beach, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

This painting was a commission from a Greystones resident from a photograph of a favourite dog digging about on the pebbly beach while a bird soared overhead. The early morning grey sky peeling back to reveal the blues made it a joy to paint.

Into the Blue Greystones seafront, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

The building of the marina has brought many changes to the coastline as the sea changes its push and pull. Many little inlets have emerged along the seafront, making little rockpools and sunbathing spots. This was painted for a young Greystones couple starting a new life away from the 'Stones.

Love on the rocks, Greystones cove, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

This is the cover of my 2021 calendar. The original painting was commissioned by someone who grew up in Greystones, now living in Germany, who wanted to capture the force of the Irish sea. My love of the waves being smashed on the rocks gave the painting its name (although I do love the Neil Diamond song too! I have painted a similar scene for an old Greystonian now living in England.

Stormy day at Greystones Cove, Co. Wicklow

My first triptych painting. A Facebook friend had posted a photo of this view at the cove providing the ideal challenge to paint it over three canvas panels. It wasn't dry on the canvas and someone called to my house to deliver something, saw it and bought it!

Until Next Time, Coolnagreina YWCA, Greystones, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

This view and place is all about me! Coolnagreina is a very special place, not just because it is the only wheelchair accessible accommodation in Greystones, but it is also the most wonderfully serene place, situated on the harbour in the heart of Greystones. Built in 1850, it provided safe and affordable accommodation for young women in domestic service and shop workers. Transformed to meet today's diverse needs it still remains consistent to its values. Growing up in Greystones the Y provided amazing summer fun with the CSSM, the highlight was the building of the most enormous sandcastle on the South Beach. At Coolnagreina I meet so many people from all over the world returning home to visit family and others on holidays or retreats. When I stay there and watch the sun go down between Little Sugarloaf and Kindlestown Wood I know that the sun will rise tomorrow and hopefully there will always be a 'tomorrow' for me to come home.
