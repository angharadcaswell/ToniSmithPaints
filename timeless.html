Timeless

My first framed painting was Nearly There – the view of the railway tracks going into the tunnel from the Bray to Greystones cliff walk. Then after I painted Walking the Walk – the view of Greystones from the cliff walk, I realised that some scenes are truly timeless. And so, the Timeless collection was born.

The Drummin Trees from Bellevue Hill, Delgany. Co. Wicklow, Ireland

Everyone from Greystones, Delgany and most of Co Wicklow recognises these iconic trees letting you know you are nearing home. In fact, during the first world war when trees were being cut down for fuel, these trees escaped such fate as they were used as a navigation aid for pilots. I was commissioned to paint this stunning scene from Bellevue Hill, by someone who lives in Delgany and wanted the view captured inside as well as when out walking.

Gone for a swim, Greystones Men's, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

Growing up in Greystones in the 1970s the Men's was the best place to swim when the tide was in. Mary Hackett had won her amazing battle to allow everyone to swim there (although it has still retained the name!). We would cycle along the seafront from the south beach, or from the harbour and cove, and quickly lean our bikes against the wall in a rush to see if the tide was perfect for diving off the board and swimming. An old friend commissioned this painting, and to bring it to life she and another friend retraced their steps parking their bikes where we would have put them. Sadly the diving board is buried under feet of sand for most of the year! I have painted this scene many times it evokes similar memories for others from a certain era.

Early Morning Swim: Greystones Men's at sunrise, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

Greystones Men's was such a special place for us growing up in the 1970s. When I was asked to capture the spirit of the memory I started with the approach down the steps to the wall when the anticipation and excitement mounted. The foliage just makes for a more magical scene. I have painted this scene twice - one painting is happily hanging in Australia, the other in Greystones.p>

Winter sunrise at Greystones south beach, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

Winter sunrises are always special as the oranges light up the dawn sky, lasting a very short time. The moment just had to be captured so cards and prints could be made.

Summer fun at Greystones Men's, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

This painting depicts summer days at the Men's in the last few years before it disappeared under a mountain of sand. This painting keeps those days and memories alive, and hangs in a local home.

Time to Think - Greystones seafront, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

I painted this commission from a photo taken in 2006. Whilst the seating along the seafront has changed greatly, this special view is timeless. I felt so at peace while painting it I use it on my banner for my Etsy shop. It is loved by the person who commissioned it.

Tomfoolery in Greystones, To the south beach arch, Greystones, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

I had painted a similar painting with two girls going down to the beach with towels rolled up under their arms when someone contacted me and said they had a similar memory but he was messing around with a friend on their bikes. So I painted him this painting. And as his name was Tom there was only one title! I have painted a similar painting for another Tom with the same memory.

Childhood Memories - To the south beach arch, Greystones, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

The person who commissioned this painting wanted to capture a childhood memory of going for a swim. I worked from a photo taken recently and asked her how she remembered it. I omitted the modern parts of the photo and painted in the bits as she remembered, consulting with her throughout. I used glass gems for the flowers to bring a bit of magic to the finished product. I have painted a similar scene for another client, and one with boys on bikes for two other clients I LOVE this scene, full of anticipation about what beach we will swim in!

Rush to the Raft at Greystones Men's, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

Every summer a raft magically appeared, perfectly positioned so we could jump off the diving board and swim out to it. It was many, many years before we realised that a very kind gentleman who lived directly opposite the Men's organised, funded and liaised with the council to make this possible, giving us youngsters a perfect summer. Thanks Mr Davy! I used about four different photos to create this painting. I have painted it a few times, and it is in homes in Greystones and Dublin.

The Way it was, Greystones Men's 2003, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

I painted this from a photo taken on the same day as 'When we we were young'. When you stood at the top of the steps you had no idea what was on the other side of the wall. It always felt calm and serene, unable to answer our questions until we descended the steps, past the hebe bushes and yucca plants and looked over the wall. Then we could see if the tide was in, if we could dive off the board, swim out to the raft, and see who else was there to hang out with.

The Poem - Greystones Men's, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

This painting was commissioned to capture the depth and intensity of teenage love. Meeting together to watch the sun rise over the Irish Sea.
