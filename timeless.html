Timeless

My first framed painting was Nearly There – the view of the railway tracks going into the tunnel from the Bray to Greystones cliff walk. Then after I painted Walking the Walk – the view of Greystones from the cliff walk, I realised that some scenes are truly timeless. And so, the Timeless collection was born.

Walking the Walk- Bray to Greystones cliff walk, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

The 9km Bray to Greystones cliff walk was built in the 1800's to facilitate the men and materials constructing the train line below. It sits lies high above the railway tracks and follows the same route with the most glorious sea views and scenery. Covered in gorse and wild flowers it's a popular tourist attraction and a daily walk/run for many locals. I painted this scene and was delighted when Cafe Grey in Greystones bought it to display in their shop. It is a timeless and iconic view.

Gone for a swim, Greystones Men's, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

Growing up in Greystones in the 1970s the Men's was the best place to swim when the tide was in. Mary Hackett had won her amazing battle to allow everyone to swim there (although it has still retained the name!). We would cycle along the seafront from the south beach, or from the harbour and cove, and quickly lean our bikes against the wall in a rush to see if the tide was perfect for diving off the board and swimming. An old friend commissioned this painting, and to bring it to life she and another friend retraced their steps parking their bikes where we would have put them. Sadly the diving board is buried under feet of sand for most of the year! I have painted this scene many times it evokes similar memories for others from a certain era.

Early Morning Swim: Greystones Men's at sunrise, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

Greystones Men's was such a special place for us growing up in the 1970s. When I was asked to capture the spirit of the memory I started with the approach down the steps to the wall when the anticipation and excitement mounted. The foliage just makes for a more magical scene. I have painted this scene twice - one painting is happily hanging in Australia, the other in Greystones.

Nearly There - on the train to Greystones, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

The coastal train ride from Dublin to Greystones is one of the top 10 things to do when visiting Ireland. The last leg of the journey from Bray to Greystones is truly spectacular. The railway line hugs the cliffs - a feat of engineering laid out by Isambard Kingdom Brunel in the 1850s. It cuts through tunnels before emerging into Greystones. As you enter the tunnels you know you were 'nearly there, nearly home. This special original painting hangs in my home as a gentle reminder of what is to come.

Greystones from the top of the 84 bus

When coming home to Greystones after a day in Dublin (or anywhere!) on the 84 bus, I always woke from a doze as soon as the bus came down through Windgates. When someone who grew up near Greystones, now living in Cyprus, asked me to capture the view I knew exactly what she meant. The haybales in D'Arcy's fields, the rolling fields reaching down to the North beach and the harbour beyond. It is a Timeless view and a wonderful memory.

Morning Glory, Wicklow mountains from Bray Head, Co, Wicklow, Ireland

Behind the yellow rapeseed fields and woods sits Little Sugarloaf, then Great Sugarloaf, with Djouce at the back. Sitting at the top of Bray Head you have this glorious view looking west, with the sea to the north, east and south. What's not to love?!

Lost on a painted sky, winter storm at Greystone South beach, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

When I was home just before the pandemic I took a photo of a stormy February day. I painted the scene using pointillism (making dots) for the sand and lots of glass gems to bring out the sparkle on the sea and sand. The painting hangs in a house in London.

Sound of silence, Greystones seafront looking to Bray Head, Co. Wicklow, Ireland.

I painted this during the last lockdown, from photo taken by Kieran McCormish. I tried to capture the silence that was felt by many people unable to be with friends and family. Painting the beautiful Greystones view filled me with hope that things will get better. I loved using unpredictable colours to bring the wall to life, and gems to lift the colour of the grass.

Winter sunrise at Greystones south beach, Co. Wicklow, Ireland

Winter sunrises are always special as the oranges light up the dawn sky, lasting a very short time. The moment just had to be captured so cards and prints could be made.
